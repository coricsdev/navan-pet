how does the navan dental protocol work?

the navan dental protocol is based on consistency and mechanics. extended chewing and natural texture help support plaque reduction over time, without relying on chemical additives or starch-based fillers.

are navan chews considered dental chews?

navan chews are not traditional molded dental treats. they are part of a natural dental protocol that relies on chewing action rather than synthetic ingredients or processed forms.

the ingredients

what ingredients are used in the navan chew protocol?

each navan chew is made from a single, clearly identified animal ingredient, with no starches, glycerin, fillers, or artificial additives.

do navan chews contain glycerin or starch?

no. navan chews do not contain glycerin, starches, or filler ingredients commonly used in many conventional dental treats.

04

usage & safety

05 questions

are navan chews safe for dogs?

navan chews are designed for supervised chewing and should be selected based on your dog's size and chewing style. like all chews, they should be given under supervision.

can navan chews replace brushing or vet dental care?

no. navan chews are intended to support everyday dental habits and complement regular veterinary dental care — not replace professional cleanings or brushing.

how often should i give my dog a navan chew?

many pet parents incorporate navan chews into their dog's routine daily or several times per week. frequency should be adjusted based on your dog's size, diet, and chewing habits.

are navan chews suitable for puppies or senior dogs?

chew suitability depends on size, texture, and individual chewing ability. pet parents should choose appropriately sized chews and consult their veterinarian if unsure.

what's your general guidance on supervision?

always supervise your dog while they chew. remove any piece small enough to be swallowed whole, and rotate chews to match your dog's current size and jaw strength.

if you notice unusual behavior, digestive issues, or have questions about your dog's specific needs, consult your veterinarian.
